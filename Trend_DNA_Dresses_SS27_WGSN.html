Season read

WGSN frames SS27 dresses as the womenswear growth engine — the largest, most stable category, reset around versatility, comfort and expressive design. The structural move is away from body-conscious toward soft architectural volume: trapeze and drop-waist column rise as heatwaves push airflow up the priority list, while bodycon declines. Four directions carry the newness — soft-volume trapeze, eclectic-resort boho, pretty-feminine prairie, and a BaRock / dark-romance craft story — across a sun-drenched, expressive palette anchored by Luminous Blue (WGSN Colour of the Year), statement reds and Power Pastels.

Trapeze minidress — the swing revival

Created by YSL in 1958 as a break from Dior's cinched waist, the trapeze swings away from the body for an unencumbered feel — re-emerging now as bodycon recedes and airflow becomes a priority. Its reference is 1960s mod (floral, eyelet, shift).

The revival rides the 60s-gamine mood feeding boho (Jean Seberg, not Woodstock), so it reads "counterculture with a bit more class" and crosses into contemporary resort.

Sources: Bridgette Raes · Who What Wear
Draped pleated column — drop-waist & elongation

A standout Summer 2026 silhouette blending 1920s flapper ease with a modern elongated line, rendered broadly — bubble-hem minis, floor-sweeping shapes, crisp white maxis, smocked versions.

FW26–27 runways confirm it: Chanel belted drop-waist to stretch the torso, Ferragamo used georgette & pleating for a long fluid line — the treatment seen in this Luminous-Blue column.

Sources: Who What Wear · Rue Sophie · MyStyleBox

The season's structural bet, and the one the data backs: as bodycon declines, the trapeze mini and drop-waist column rise. Architectural yet easy — sculptural shapes that maximise movement, sit across generations, and read as newness without losing commerciality.

Boho halter — the 2026 "Bold Boho" renaissance

Boho's revival is a defining movement of 2026 — Bella Hadid in Chloé, plus Chloé, Zimmermann, Ulla Johnson. It has shifted to "Bold Boho": punchy mixed prints, bandana motifs, gathered fabrics, lace — more punk than pretty.

This patchwork halter is textbook Bold Boho, and analysts expect the renaissance past 2026 — matching WGSN's call that boho runs into SS27 toward "Bohemian Archive."

Sources: Who What Wear · Marie Claire UK · Metromode
Sarong & crochet — resort beyond the beach

For 2026 the sarong sheds its beach-only reputation: luxe silk, oversized tropical prints, tied at the hip / wrapped as a halter dress / draped — styled into the city over shirts and trousers.

Crochet & fringe are core to the boho-craft revival, giving the hand-made, artisanal feel the trend now demands (and a natural elevated price tier).

Sources: Who What Wear — sarong 2026 · Rue Sophie — fringe & crochet

WGSN flags boho as a key lifestyle trend to invest in, continuing into SS27 and evolving toward "Bohemian Archive." Eclectic Resort delivers it as dresses — mixed/scarf prints, hybrid halter/slip, sarong draping, crochet craft — for breathable, beach-to-occasion versatility.

Prairie tiered floral — refined, not costume watch

Cottagecore has matured in 2026: the midi prairie stays the cornerstone but reads "less costume-like, more refined" — high-neck, button-front, billowy sleeves, tiered skirts in lightweight cotton/linen, ditsy florals in dusty rose & sage.

Counter-signal: some retail data shows "prairie dress"/"puff-sleeve" searches down ~30% YoY as doll-like styling cedes to minimalism. Hence Expand/peak, not Invest — keep refined, grounded in garden-tone palette.

Sources: Sew Mama Sew · Maurices · Accio (decline)
Sheer floral drop-waist — the organza pivot

Sheer dressing is now more refined and less revealing — layered over a slip. The SS27 direction pivots to organza for subtler translucency rather than fully see-through.

Jacquemus' Spring 2027 runway showed exactly this — airy organza dresses with a gently translucent effect — validating this sheer floral drop-waist as a forward, wearable read.

Statement-red raw-edge — deconstruction & the artisanal hand

Deconstruction is the dominant SS26 narrative: exposed seams, raw/frayed (déchiré) edges, flipped linings, hybrid garments — Glenn Martens a reference point. It reads as a hunger for authenticity against AI-generated perfection, with "the work of the hand" positioned as luxury.

This statement-red look — high neck, frayed raw-edge placket — sits in that craft story and pairs with dark-romance codes (sheer, boudoir lace/slip, deep reds/black).

Sources: WWD — Deconstruction SS26 · Faz — raw edges · Accio — Dark Romance
Broderie cutwork — the eyelet that won't quit

Broderie anglaise is defining summer dressing: cutwork eyelets + raised embroidery on breathable cotton, adding light-catching dimension. Designers are sharpening silhouettes and proportion — volume on tiered/maxi, or as a trim on minimalist pieces.

It's seasonally fluid (layered under knits, with boots), supporting WGSN's cross-seasonality mandate; here cutwork + tie-cuffs double as modest, breathable coverage.

Sources: Who What Wear — broderie 2026 · Woman & Home · Harper's Bazaar

The directional, design-led bet — WGSN's BaRock and #DarkRomance threads plus the "expressive, maximalist details" mandate (lace, scalloped edges, fringing, raw edges, metallics) that justify higher price positioning. Romantic-meets-rock craft on slip/column bases, in oxblood and statement red. Test in occasion; scale the craft details across safer themes.
