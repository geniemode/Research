## ANSWER (text)

For Spring/Summer 2027, women's dresses are positioned as a growth engine for the
apparel market, with a strong focus on versatility, comfort, and expressive design.
The season is defined by a blend of nostalgic references, maximalist details, and
adaptable silhouettes that cater to evolving consumer needs for both style and practicality.

### Need to Know
- Dresses remain the largest and most stable category in womenswear, primed for innovation and newness.
- Key themes: Surreal Suburbia, BaRock, and a resurgence of 1920s nostalgia, alongside ongoing 1970s and 1980s influences.
- Functionality, comfort, and cross-seasonality prioritized — silhouettes that transition between occasions and climates.
- Modest fashion and resortwear are major growth areas → demand for breathable fabrics and modest-friendly coverage.
- Expressive, design-led details and maximalist accents are central, moving away from pared-back basics.

### Opportunities
| Trend/Style | TIP Stage | Business Action |
|---|---|---|
| Trapeze mini dress | Invest | Anchor as key colour/print vehicle; co-ord for layering & beachwear |
| Drop-waist column dress | Invest | Relaxed, loose-sleeve, over-the-knee for broad appeal |
| BaRock fit-and-flare dress | Test | Romantic-meets-rock with bold details and fabric contrasts |

| Shirt and slip dresses | Invest | Versatile, size-inclusive, transseasonal |

### Proof Points
- Column and fit-and-flare dresses forecasted for increased demand; bodycon styles in decline.
- Trapeze mini and boho halter dresses are season-defining, with strong cross-category potential.
- Prairie and tunic dresses leverage #PrettyFeminine and #Minimalist trends — sheers, lace, openwork.
- Shirt styling and slip dresses continue to rise, supported by catwalk and social data.
- Maximalist details — lace trims, scalloped edges, fringing, metallics — key for product elevation.

### WGSN Data Highlights
- Shirt styling up **+7.5ppt** on S/S 26 catwalks vs S/S 25.
- #HalterNeck posts up **+2.4ppt** on S/S 26 catwalks vs S/S 25.
- #SlipDress up **+1.4ppt**, with #DarkRomance and BaRock themes forecast to continue.
- Draped styling: #Minimalist on S/S 26 catwalks **+2ppt** (WGSN Data, S/S 26 vs S/S 25).

### Strategies (Buying, Design, Marketing)
- Build dress assortments around versatile, modest-friendly silhouettes with breathable, sun-drenched palettes.
- Use dresses as key vehicles for colour and print innovation — Power Pastels, Luminous Blue, statement reds.
- Prioritise adjustable, body-responsive features (soft ties, smocking, layering) for comfort and fit flexibility.
- Incorporate expressive, maximalist details (lace, fringing, beading, metallics) for higher price positioning.
- Position key styles (trapeze mini, boho halter, prairie, tunic) for occasion + everyday; co-ords and layering for cross-seasonality.

### In Action
- Trapeze mini: co-ord with shirts/trousers for cooler months, or beach cover-up.
- Drop-waist column: relaxed fit, loose sleeves, over-the-knee for multi-generational appeal.
- Prairie: delicate details, sheers, ditsy florals, waist focus.
- Boho halter: spliced construction, playful colour, hybrid details.
- Tunic: knitted openwork, lace, pointelle for breezy, beyond-the-beach versatility.

### Key Consumer Profiles
- Modest fashion consumers seeking coverage and comfort.
- Resortwear shoppers prioritising breathable, sun-drenched fabrics.
- Young and older markets drawn to relaxed, timeless silhouettes.
- Maximalists and expressive dressers investing in statement details and colour.

### Key Markets
- North America and EMEA primary focus regions; global resonance for key silhouettes/details.

### STEPIC Forecasts
- S/S 27: Empowered Play, Geo-Logic, Kintentional — intentional design, community, expressive individuality.
- Big Ideas 2028: Maximalism, personal style development, investment in statement pieces.
